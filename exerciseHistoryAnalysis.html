Exercise 1
calendar_month 23.02.2026 / 24.02.2026

We started off the exercise class with a short revision of the lecture content about logic and we solved some exercises related to transforming natural language into formulas and formulas to natural language. Afterwards we went over the portion of the lecture about sets and we solved some exercises about proving set equality or equivalence between statements about sets. Then we mooved on to discussing the definitions of upper/lower bouns of a set, the supremum, infimum, minimum and maximum of a set. We solved an exercise in which for a given set we needed to determine the supremum and the infumum of the set. Finally, we used the multiplication/addition/order axioms to prove some statements about real numbers.

Exercise 3
calendar_month 09.03.2026 / 10.03.2026

We started off the class by revising the theory taught in the lecture. Then, we moved on to showing that a sequence converges using the epsilon definition seen in the lecture. After that, we computed the limits of different types of sequences. First, we considered sequences in which the index n we iterate over was in the exponent, and then where n was under a square root or a cube root. Afterwards, we solved several exercises in which the sequence whose limit we were looking for was defined as an expression of another sequence that converges. Finally, we solved several exercises that used the definition of the number e as the limit of the sequence (1+1/n)^n.
